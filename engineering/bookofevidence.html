Background

The Picker Engineering Program’s B.S. in engineering science is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET requiring the assessment and documentation of student outcomes and their corresponding performance indicators. Artifacts are scored on a scale of EAMU.

Student Outcome (1): an ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems by applying principles of engineering, science, and mathematics

(1)a: The student solves a complex engineering problem that requires mathematical skill and principles from engineering science (e.g., mechanics, fluids, circuits, thermodynamics).

Score: E. Artifact Source: EGR 351 Case Study 1

(1)b: The student transforms a complex system into a simplified mathematical model and articulates the impact of simplifying assumptions.

Score: E. Artifact Source: EGR 390fe Final Project Paper

(1)c: The student identifies, formulates, and solves a complex engineering problem that requires mathematical skill and principles from engineering science (e.g., mechanics, fluids, circuits, thermodynamics).

Score: E. Artifact Source: EGR 351 Learning Workshop
